What is my project?

My project is to simulate the concentration distributions of electrons and holes within a MOSFET and the dynamics within the MOSFET along with variation to the voltage imposed to the gate electrode via the Multiphysics simulation software COMSOL.

What are my research target and research method?

Th research target is to produce a clear and comprehensive understanding of how MOSFET works as a non-linear system inside a circuit. And, the importance of the research is that the result could be used to seek multiple optimization methods to adapt the MOSFET model into different variations for various application scenarios based on the relationships between the parameters and the performance of MOSFET deduced.

The research question of this project is how the carrier concentrations inside a MOSFET will behave under changing gate voltage. The geometric model of MOSFET used to simulate is shown in Image 1. The blue area in the image represents the donor doping area with free electrons under base; the purple areas represent the acceptor doping areas with free holes under source and drain; the green area represents the donor doping area in the background; and the black line segments on the surface of MOSFET are base electrode, source electrode, gate electrode, and drain electrode.

The parameters used in the simulation for the model are listed in Table 1.

All procedures of the investigation will occur on the platform of COMSOL Multiphysics. The steps of the procedure are shown as the following:

  1. Construct the geometric structure of the MOSFET in COMSOL Multiphysics with all parameters set.
  2. Set up the characteristics and parameters for the materials used in a MOSFET and designate different doping areas for the MOSFET.
  3. Set up physical contacts required to simulate the interactions between the electrodes and the doping areas
  4. Define the size of mesh to ensure a high resolution and high accuracy of the simulation.
  5. Add an auxiliary sweep from the range of 0 to -3V with a footstep of 0.1V and run a stationary simulation to the model constructed.
  6. Plot the distribution of carrier concentrations under different gate voltages and the graph of the change in the carrier concentration in the area below the gate electrode with the change of gate voltage.

What was the biggest challenge that I encountered? How did I overcome it?

The biggest challenge is my unfamiliarity with the detailed theories about semiconductors at first. Though I have been appealed to such a topic, the lack of systematic learning to this field impeded me to delve into this project. One way I attempted to overcome this problem was to frequently discuss the theory part and various models with my tutor Dr. Kou. And, from those discussions, I managed to build up a relatively comprehensive knowledge frame to the field of semiconductors. Also, during my spare time, I looked up for essays and articles about MOSFET and other semiconductor components to enrich my knowledge to the field, so that I have become more comfortable with the physics and math in the project.

What are my research result(s) and conclusion(s)?

The simulation in COMSOL shows that the MOSFET works as predicted: while the MOSFET is applied with a negative threshold voltage on the ground electrode, the electric field created by the voltage on the ground electrode will attract holes from the base of the MOSFET to the surface of it between the source electrode and the drain electrode, and form a hole tunnel to render the component conductive. Under the parametrization setting applied in the simulation, the model has a threshold voltage of approximately -2.0V.
